Uncertainty Types

Uncertainties of geographical data can belong to basic three types (Longley et al. 2010). The first is the uncertainty of location data, also known as positional uncertainty. An example would be, not knowing the exact location of a geographic feature, a tree. The second is the uncertainty in attribute data, also known as attribute uncertainty. Attribute uncertainty can be expressed for an example, as the inexactness of the temperature for a given area. Thirdly, what is known as temporal uncertainty is the inexactness of the temporal dimension of events that occur and vary through time.
